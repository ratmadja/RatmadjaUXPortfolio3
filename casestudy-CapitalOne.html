Elevating the Innovation Team to the Customer View

Embracing the role of a Strategic Designer in bridging across multiple product initiatives, and rallying the entire Customer Experience Team to redefine their identity and purpose for forward momentum.

ROLE
  • As the Sole Designer for an entire Customer Experience Team, I support 3 Product Innovation Initiatives, and special assignments on an case-by-case basis.
  • I led the planning, execution, and synthesis for 2 Customer Empathy Research initiatives—where I effectively engaged the Product, Operations, and Design Research partners in order to maximize our learnings.
IMPACT
  • Led the Customer Research, Interaction & Interface Design, Customer Validation, and Build-Support efforts for 4 Digital Tools & Platforms—each with its own unique set of customer needs, design constraints, and interface design systems.
  • Facilitated cross-functional requirements-gathering and scope discussions between 3 Product Managers to ensure alignment of 3 Product Roadmaps..
  • Upleveled the product innnovation agenda to a unified Customer Vision that bridged the 3 key innovation initiatives at a design strategy level—so as to ensure a seamless customer service experience across the digital product ecosystem.
OUTPUT
  • Produced Customer-backed designs for digital tools and experiences for 4 Digital Tools and Platforms, 3 of which are internal Associate-facing, and 1 of which is external Business Customer-facing. Each digital tool had their own unique set of stakeholders, user groups, and design challenges.
  • Led the creation of, and secured Leadership buy-in for the unified Customer Vision—where each Business, Product, Operations, and Tech initiatives are ladderred up to the key Customer Problem and the overarching Customer Vision.
  • Coordinated the Research Planning, Execution and Synthesis of learnings for 2 Customer Research initiatives, and shared the key insights and actionable learnings, and raw customer interviews to the Business, Tech, and Operations partners.

The Why

A large part of my work is having the ability to discern where people are struggling, and where people are being excluded from opportunities for a better way to live, work, and play.

At a meta-level, here are how I see the purpose of the key initiatives I am presently engaged in at Capital One:

Initiative 1

The first initiative is about giving our internal Associates back their freedom after being subjected to manual tools that compound human errors, and make it difficult to serve the Customer in the way that the Customer would like to be served. Our team is on a journey to build a modern platform and system of record for all internal Operations Associates. Ultimately, our intent is to hold the same high bar of quality for the tools we build for our internal Associates, as we do for our external Customers. Additionally, this modernization will position our Associates to reap the benefits of AI and Machine Learning in the future.

Initiative 2

The second initiative is about safeguarding the relationship between Capital One and our valued Business Customers when it comes to difficult situations where miscommunications arise, and resulted in unmet expectations on both sides. To that end, we are laying the groundwork for a more consistent set of internal processes and procedures that complement the modern tool we are building for our business customer. The goal is to increase our Business Customers' perception of the value that Capital One delivers—which will strengthen the existing partnership.

Initiative 3

The third initiative is about empowering our business analysts with a better tool to help them make sense of fluctuations in the marketplace, particularly in a fast-moving environment exacerbated by the Covid19 Pandemic. The goal is to build a tool that will enhance the speed of our analysts' work, enhance their throughput with better quality data, and ultimately empower them to secure the confidence of senior leadership when presenting their strategic recommendations.
